What does the score mean?


The lower the score the better!

The scoring system is called the brier score

When you predict you make an estimate of the probability of an event that is between 0 - 100%
when an event happens, the probability of that event happening is now 100%. Conversely, 0% if it did not happen.


The Brier score is equal to the difference between your estimate and its real probability squared.
So if you estimate there to be a 70% chance of X happening and it does happen:

   Score = ( real probability - your estimate )^2
       (100% - 70%)^2
       (0.30)^2
   Score = 0.09
